2. Separate the work into three buckets

The purpose is not perfect categorization. It is to distinguish obligation, improvement, and invention.

Bucket 1
Protect

Keeps revenue, delivery, credibility, and basic operations moving.

Bucket 2
Grow

Improves how NoLie markets, sells, delivers, and operates.

Bucket 3
Invent

Creates future capabilities, products, proof points, or revenue.

Operating work still consumes capacity, but it should not compete in the same vote as a new strategic bet.

3. Agree how much capacity each bucket receives

Use directional percentages—not timesheets. The total must equal 100%.

70%
20%
10%
Total: 100%

Use this rule going forward

When a new initiative enters a full bucket, it must replace something already there.

This converts “Could we?” into the more useful question: “What would we stop, delay, or delegate to make room?”

4. Choose the strategic bets

Select no more than two: one primary investment and, only if capacity allows, one quick win.

0 of 2 selected
Selection does not mean every task is approved. It means this initiative earns the right to be scoped, staffed, and measured during the next 90 days.
